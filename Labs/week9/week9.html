Task 2.2: Compare chroma histograms

String Quartet in A Major, K. 464

Cod Demotika

Dresscode

For the three tracks, I decided to compare the histograms for chroma features. The chroma-based feature helps identify pitches that differ by an octave, corresponding to 12 pitches in standard Western music: C, C#, D, D#, E, F, F#, G, G#, A, A# and B. It can serve as a tool to demonstrate variations in timbre. Since I have chosen the three tracks from different genres, namely classical, folk, and death metal, each piece's timbre is expected to be quite distinctive. Indeed, the histograms for the chroma feature match the expectation. It's interesting to see that the intensity of each of the 12 chromas of the octave is relatively high in Dresscode compared to Mozart's String Quartet in A Major, K. 464. Additionally, Cod Demotika was played in a very 'static' manner, consisting of a melodic that repeats. In contrast, These characteristics indeed match my experience when I listened to them. However, I found it surprising that some distributions of pitches in Dresscode, which is death metal, and Cod Demotika, which is folk, resemble. If not looking at the histograms, I expected that the pitches of the three tracks are completely different without any similarities.
