Task 1: jSymbolic

First, I exported You'll Be Back as a MIDI file. Then, I followed instructions and generated a jSymbolic of the piece. I have selected a range of features, and here is the CSV file.

The table includes the basic features of the analysis, such as the number of pitches, number of pitch classes, range, mean pitch, and mean pitch class. There are some interesting results from these basic features. For example, while the number of pitch classes is 12, there is only one common pitch class of this music piece. The same applies to the number of common pitches. There are only 2 common pitches in 49 pitches. The result means that there are 2 pitches account individually for at least 20% of all notes. The range is 55, indicating the different in semitones between the highest and lowest pitches. Further, I also selected some other features that I think are interesting and relevant to my work. For instance, the pitch skewness of my piece is -0.8347, demonstrating that an asymmetrical pitch distribution to the left of the mean pitch. Further, the first MIDI pitch is 55, which shows that the first pitch is G3. The data also shows that the middle register appears to be more significant than bass or high registers in this music piece.
